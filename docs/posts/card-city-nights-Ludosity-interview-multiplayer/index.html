Welcome to Frivolity

Card City Nights 2 multiplayer with Joel Nyström from Ludosity

If you’re a fan of card games, you probably heard of Card City Nights by now. Released back in 2014, the quirky card game was a mash-up featuring characters from Ludosity’s other games, reimagined as card players. With an interesting core mechanic, stellar soundtrack and spiffy characters, the game was well-received and still provides a refreshing experience 3 years later. So naturally, fans are eagerly awaiting the sequel!

The upcoming Card City Nights 2 launches September 1 on Steam. The game is familiar, yet new, with 200+ cards and some big changes to core mechanics. And, what's even more exciting, it brings a much-awaited feature: online multiplayer.

Players will place cards on the same game board.

CCN 2 combines traditional deck-building and card battle gameplay with a RPG-like story-line. You’re traveling aboard Starship Frivolity, where everyone’s favourite pastime is - you guessed it - playing cards! You battle zany characters in a quest to achieve fortune & glory. Oh, and collect all the cards! Then, you duke it out online with other players.

The core gameplay features an unique card chaining mechanic. Players seek to activate cards by linking their chain points. Once you link 3 cards, they resolve. When a card is resolved, it may trigger abilities, and then it leaves play.

That sounds simple, but it gets a lot more complex once you start playing. Factors such as card placement, timings, chain points and card abilities ensure a variety of tactics. The game promises a diverse metagame, close to the traditional aggro-combo-control scale of other CCGs.

The Potato — #1 card in the beta

Developers Ludosity are tiny but strong: while just a small team of 6, they've been around for almost 10 years, and have over 20 published titles. They all feature an unique feel, "lighthearted and funny with a cartoony style".

The team had a blast balancing the meta in their first multiplayer beta, which ended a few weeks ago. So I asked Joel Nyström to take a little time and give us some insight around the game, the beta and what to expect in multiplayer come September.

  1. Vlad: So Card City Nights 2 is out in September, excited about that?
  2. Joel: Yes, very excited! The reception so far has been very good, and we have a lot more stuff to reveal yet =)
  3. Vlad: Will we play a much different game at launch, than now in the beta?
  4. Joel: The multiplayer matches will not change significantly from what you saw in the beta, except for improved menus and UX. However there's also the whole single-player adventure that we haven't revealed yet!
  5. V: Multiplayer is very much anticipated, at least judging from the player activity in the beta. What were the challenges to create a multiplayer component to Card City Nights?
  6. J: The original CCN was probably not very balanced for competitive play, I think some cards were simply just better than others in that game. Because it was only a single-player adventure, we balanced it according to a difficulty curve that fit with the story arch. With CCN2 we try to balance the cards so that there will be no dominant strategies and every card can fill a useful role in a deck.
  7. V: We definitely saw better balance in the mp beta. Speaking of, was there a meta-game already forming?
  8. J: The community would often post on the forums what decks/strategies they perceived as dominant, but the dominant strategies changed several times over a short 10-day period, and looking at the stats, the numbers aren't quite showing the same picture! So balancing the meta is something that's going extremely interesting once the game is live.
  9. V: How different is it to test it with real players, versus just an internal test group? Was there a lot of unexpected stuff happening?
  10. J: We did the beta precisely to be on stand-by to quickly fix as many things as we could during the 10-day beta. It was a lot of fun, and very intense! Working live against an audience is a blast!
  11. V: Any critical balance changes that you've identified in the beta?
  12. J: Yes, in the initial version we might have gone a little too far with the unusual and weird mechanics, while neglecting the core mechanic of connecting chains. This was re-balanced during the beta.
  13. V: Will we see some single-player content closer to launch?
  14. J: There won't be any more betas before launch now, but we'll start revealing more of the adventure in trailers, screenshots and blog-posts soon! Also, catch us at Gamescom in Cologne this August and I'll personally show you =)

With all that in mind, multiplayer should be a total blast! The last beta gave us a taste. Focused on multiplayer, beta #2 was open to 1000 players, who quickly generated a meta that shifted quite a bit during the course of the event. As a result, the devs released a couple of balance changes in short succession.

Princess Remedy proved to be a great control card

I loved the fact that new decks emerged every day: at first, the Frog deck ruled. Then, players started countering it with control decks, then by using the best princess there is: Princess Remedy . In the last few days, people noticed that a fatigue archetype is a contender for "top deck". This can only mean good news for the finished game — everyone wants a dynamic meta with multiple deck types to explore.

Card City Nights comes out on September 1 on Steam.
