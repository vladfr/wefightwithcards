#discover

Hand of Fate 2
Steam / Win, Mac, Linux

Hand of Fate 2 is hot off the press and picks up where the first Hand of Fate left off. One of the neatest aspects of Hand of Fate 2, like the first, is the visuals and overall feel of the game. The game is played in front of a dealer who is sort of like the narrator but is also much more. I found the aesthetics totally awesome in the first one, for instance the first time you see the dealer he swirls all the cards up in the air and then sway them into a deck.

I played the first Hand of Fate but it really didn’t hold my interest past the first play session. I could clearly see all the cool stuff going on in it and could see why so many others raved about it, but I personally didn’t enjoy it as much as many other card games. It alternates back and forth between a deck of cards and a live aRPG-style fighting game. I think my main problem was that the fighting game portion felt pretty underwhelming. Regardless, I definitely recommend any card game fan, at the very least, go take a look at Hand of Fate 2's steam page. The advertising describes many new features that seem like they could be amazing additions to what I had played before and I'm sure it will be enjoyable to many of you out there.

-Leo Elworth (CHILLEO@gmail.com)


Mythgard
browser game / beta
Well this is a novel idea: a full-fledged card game in the browser. Mythgard will feel familiar, and also different: it has creatures and spells, but no mana. Instead, you sacrifice any card to get 1 mana, once per turn. I haven't played much but it looks promising.
