Get your Gwent on!

Everything you need to get started with Gwent

This week, everybody can join in the fun & games of Gwent, the Witcher card came.

Game intro

Gwent is the acclaimed Witcher 3 card game, and Open Beta just started for Playstation 4, PC/Windows and XBox One.

Lead one of the five iconic factions and outsmart your opponent in a best-out-of-3 match. To win a match, you need to score more points than your opponent. To do that, you build a strong army, attack enemy units, sabotage the opposing side and use weather to gain an advantage.

Simple, right?

Well, hold on! In each match you'll have 10 cards, and you only draw 2 additional cards in Round 2, and just 1 card in case of a Round 3 tie-breaker. This means that sometimes, in order to secure a big advantage in Round 3, you'll need to concede.

Decks and guides to get started

The first thing you will do in the game is to complete the Single player Challenges. These will get you a few Kegs and Scraps so you can start building your collection. But most importantly, you will unlock all the faction leaders, which give you lots more options to build decks.

What starter deck to choose?

If you like Elves & Dwarves, I've had success with Francesca — this is the starter Scoia'tael deck. The game builds this deck for you as part of the deck-building Challenge. All you have to do is pick the Scoia'tael faction.

The main strategy is to dig for Mahakam Defender, because it is resilient and will remain on the board on Rounds 2 and 3. Then, you buff the Defenders with Swallow potion and your other cards. Francesca really helps here, because she allows you to mulligan for buffs and other cards you need.

Other cool buff combos are Mahakam Guard with Decoy, so you can buff twice, and Barclay Els to find your Dwarves.

Bad Weather?

Sure, this strategy is pretty bad against weather decks. The AI doesn't play much removal, so generally your Mahakam Defenders are safe. But you'll run into trouble with the Monsters. The key to beating them is to forget the Defenders and just dig for First Light. If you can keep the skies clear, your buffs should net you more points.

I don't like elves

If that's true then you'll love monsters. May I recommend a basic Weather deck? The idea is to use Weather cards to debuff your opponent while you play stuff on your side. Simple, mischievous, fun! Check the playlist below for a video guide on the deck, as well as videos on other starter decks to play.

I have a keg, now what?

Each keg will give you 4 cards, and let you choose a fifth. I recommend choosing a faction and sticking with it — this way, you'll be able to improve your chosen Starter deck.

If you're lucky and get to choose from 3 Gold cards, you may not know which ones to pick. Luckily, the community built a Keg Helper tool — just enter the 3 cards and it will show you which one to choose, based on card ratings from the community.
