Shadowverse is a manga-style digital CCG available on mobile and Steam. The game took the world by storm, counting over 14 million downloads.

  • Set Rotation!
  • New class: Portalcraft
  • All changes December 28
  • Plus a new set!
  • In-game Tournaments in 2018

Big news this week for Shadowverse players: developer CyGames showcased their set of announcements, dubbed "Shadowverse Next", which are bound to shake up the game in December.

Shadowverse is gearing up for it's 7th set and with it, we'll see some major changes to game modes, as well as a whole new class.

More on Shadowverse and other games in our latest issue »

New class: Portalcraft

Yuwan and the all-new Portalcraft class will arrive on December 28. Yuwan is a vengeful warrior who has the power to traverse dimensions, and will fight alongside Arisa and the others to save the world.

The core class mechanic is Resonate — cards with Resonate can activate special abilities and effects when there is an even number of cards in your deck.

Right alongside that are Artifact cards – token cards you can add either to your deck, or to your hand, so you can more easily activate Resonate cards.

Rotation and Unlimited

With the release of the 7th Shadowverse set in December, you'll be able to play in two game modes: Rotation and Unlimited.

Rotation will only allow decks made with cards from the latest 5 sets, as well as most Basic cards. This is an idea often found in popular games such as Magic: the Gathering or Hearthstone. Once the new set is out, Shadowverse will have over 1000 cards. The Set Rotation will keep the meta fresh, and make the game friendlier for new players.

The Rotation format will always feature only the latest 5 sets.


If you're attached to your collection, don't fret! Unlimited mode will allow all cards in the game. This is OK in theory, as it doesn't alienate veteran players, because it provides a game mode which rewards them for their knowledge of the game. However, what happens in other card games is that this "full" mode becomes a fringe area of the game, as most players tend to focus on the newest cards. I guess we'll have to wait and see how it plays out in Shadowverse.

Tournament mode

Finally, Shadowverse will include an automated tournament mode, a feature sorely missing from other competitive card games.

Tournaments are coming in 2018. "This will be an in-game tournament that anyone can join, regardless of rank. There will be preliminaries matches to determine which players make it to the finals to earn exclusive rewards."
