Kobolds and Catacombs is the latest Hearthstone expansion, launched on December 7. It features a sleuth of new cards that are bound to shake the meta.

Among them are the new Legendary weapons, powerful one-of's that promise to bring back weapon-hate cards, and Spellstones, cards which get free upgrades as the match progresses.

But I thought I'd take a look at the new Ladder staples: cards that you'll see time and again on the Ladder. You know, like Bonemare.

We'll exclude Legendaries, since they're a one-off, and let's face it — you know that list will be just {{< card "Patches" "hs" >}} (yes, I know he's not a Kobold).

5. Dragons

Dragon Priest just became stronger with the addition of a good board clear with a 3/3 body. Here's the Board Breaker extraordinaire — get ready to be sick of seeing 3 of these every match, courtesy of our old friend {{< card "Netherspite Historian" "hs">}}.

Board Breaker

4. Card draw

{{< card "Kobold Librarian" "hs">}} is a one drop which draws you a card when you play it. {{< card "Loot Hoarder" "hs" >}} sees play everywhere, so I fully expect to see this little guy in every Warlock deck ever. It fits perfectly into the Control Warlock strategy, and the 2 attack makes it relevant for Zoo as well.

3. Flanking 2-for-1

Here's a card that's easy to spot: a removal spell that also gives you a body is rare to come by. This wolf fits perfectly into the new Spell Hunter archetype, but you'll be seeing it almost everywhere.

2. Exploding Secret

The new Mage secret was tearing up the ladder in the first days of Kobolds, and it's not showing any sign of letting up. {{< card "Explosive Runes" "hs">}} is exactly what Mage needed to close the gap: it offers both board control and a little damage at times — and with Jaina, every bit helps to get her opponent to that dangerous below 10 health zone, a.k.a burn range.

1. Shiny!

{{< card "Cavern Shinyfinder" "hs" >}} will only ever find one shiny weapon, and that's {{< card "Kingsbane" "hs" >}} — again and again and again.

0. Corridor Creeper

Bonus! Oh and such a great bonus it is! Minions that can be played for 0-mana always see play. I think {{< card "Corridor Creeper" "hs" >}} is the first one that fits an Aggro-style deck. So yes, we'll be sick of seeing it everywhere.
